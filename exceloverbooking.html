FLight Over-Booking Analysis Project

For this project, I used my Data Analytics skills to evaluate flight data for a fictional carrier called 'Green Airways'.
Green Airways operates 3 daily flights from Dublin to London, each with a capacity of 100 passengers.
The flight data for 1 year was inspected to optimize for profit while minimising the cost of compensating "bumped" passengers.
Overbooking is a common practice to compensate for "no-shows", but it comes with its own risks and costs.

Input data was properly formatted and did not require any data cleaning or processing prior to data analysis

Data Processing:Part B

The goal of this part of the project was to create a "Monte-Carlo" simulation to identify strategies for overbooking passengers on the Dublin to London flights based on 1 and 3 overbooked passengers. The simulation took into account different demand and 'bump' costs for different times of the day 11am, 3pm and 7pm.

The Monte Carlo Simulation, I used various Excel functions:
- Rand to create 1092 random probabilities.
- norm.inv to calculate the inverse of the normal cumulative distribution for the specified mean and standard deviation calculated in part A, to simulate 'Demand' and 'No Shows'
- Average, Median, Maximum and Minimum to calculate these values for the simulated data.
